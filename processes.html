Rules Explainer

Game Processes

How dice pools are built for travel and recovery — and the six phases of Kronos's end-of-round turn

Selecting Time-Travel Dice

Agent Turn

When an agent time-travels, the game assembles a dice pool from the destination. The agent then rolls the pool, locking useful dice between rolls until the trip succeeds or fails.

1

Core destination dice

The destination always adds a Ring die, a Base die and a Planet die. A Direction die joins the pool only when the trip crosses between eras.

RingBase PlanetDirection (cross-era)
2

Bonus dice

Extra dice are granted by circumstance: a Portal die if the agent starts next to a portal, an Adjacency die if the destination is adjacent, a TimeLord die for TimeLord agents, and a Hazard (Baal) die whenever the Main Timeline is at −6 or lower.

PortalAdjacency TimeLordHazard
3

Roll and lock

The player rolls the assembled pool up to four times, locking dice they want to keep between rolls. Wild faces substitute for any required value. Two skull faces mean the trip fails.

See the Dice Reference for every travel die and its six faces.

Selecting Recovery Dice

Agent Turn

When an agent attempts to recover an article, the dice pool is built from the article's required recovery factors — Archaeology, Security, Cyber, Psychic, Medical and Historian.

1

Read the required factors

Every Item, Crystal and Artifact lists the recovery factors needed to restore it. These determine which faces the dice pool must be able to produce.

ASC PMH
2

Choose colour dice

Colour dice (Red, Blue, Green, Black, White) are selected so the pool can cover every required factor between them. Each colour die carries a different mix of factor faces.

3

Add bonus dice

A TimeLord die is added for TimeLord agents, a Hazard (Baal) die when the timeline is −6 or lower, and an Alpha die for the AutoAlpha bonus, as applicable.

TimeLordHazard Alpha
4

Roll and lock

The same mechanic applies — roll up to four times, locking dice between rolls. Wilds substitute for any factor until all required factors are matched.

The Articles Reference lists the required factors for every article in the game.

End of Round — Kronos's Turn

6 Phases

After all agents have acted, Kronos takes his turn. The six phases resolve in strict order, ending with win/loss checks.

1

Steal

Kronos rolls 2d6 for each artifact on the board and steals it on a total of 9 or higher, after modifiers:

ModifierValue
Non-portal location−5
Ring 1 — adjacent to the Vortex+3
Difficulty — Hard / Easy+2 Hard · −2 Easy
Crystal excess at HQ+ excess
Timeline tier (timeline ÷ 5, truncated)+ tier
Protected — agent present−4
Guarded−2
2

Empower

Artifacts that Kronos holds may become empowered. An empowered artifact deals more timeline damage than an ordinary one.

3

Timeline Control

Each artifact Kronos holds pushes the Main Timeline down — by −1, or by −2 if that artifact is empowered.

4

Crystal Light

Crystal Light uses a 3-tier system. Crystals deposited at HQ give a flat +1 to the timeline (+2 if empowered). Crystals in the field at their correct Time Location roll 2d6 vs a target of 9 — success gives +1. Crystals carried by agents or at wrong locations contribute nothing.

5

Time Collapse

Kronos may shorten the game by advancing the Singularity, bringing the final round closer.

6

Portal Rotation

Active portals shift to new locations on the board, changing where agents can travel cheaply next round.

The net timeline adjustment for the round is Timeline Control (Kronos's downward push) minus Crystal Light (the players' upward push). After the six phases resolve, the game checks the win and loss conditions: players win if the Main Timeline is positive and a player has cleared all blots; Kronos wins if the round limit is reached with the timeline still negative.
