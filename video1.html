Welcome to Kairos: The Big Picture

Runtime: ~8 minutes  ·  Topic: Board, Trackers, Win & Loss

What This Video Covers

Start here. Kairos is a semi-cooperative 1–4 player game — the team wins or loses together, but the player with the most Kairos at game end is the individual winner. This video tours the hex board (36 Time Locations across 3 rings, plus the central Vortex), introduces the four core trackers, and explains the two ways the game can end: your team pushes the Main Timeline positive with all blots cleared, or Kronos drags it into darkness when time runs out.

Key Points

  • The Board: 36 Time Locations on a hex grid — an inner ring of 6, middle ring of 12, outer ring of 18, plus the central Vortex. Locations span two eras: Past (history) and Future (space).
  • Kairos Track: Your team's score. Increases when you recover articles and complete actions. Printed on the board from 0 upward.
  • Blots: Dark marks on the board that must all be cleared before the game can be won. Removing a blot typically requires depositing an Item at HQ.
  • Main Timeline: The central win/lose dial. Starts at 0. Crystals push it positive; Kronos drags it negative. Win when it reaches positive territory with no blots. If Kronos pushes it to the minimum, or the Singularity arrives, the game is lost.
  • Paradox Counter: Tracks timeline instability. Rises when items pile up or events fire, falls when items are deposited. When it maxes out, the team loses immediately.
  • Turn Structure: Each round all agents take turns (any order), then the Kronos phase runs its 6 sub-phases. Rounds are tracked by the Kairos marker; if it reaches the end before you win, the game is lost.
  • Win Condition: Main Timeline in positive territory with zero blots remaining — checked before Kronos acts each round.
  • Loss Conditions: Kronos moves the Main Timeline to the negative limit, Paradox Counter maxes out, the Singularity arrives, or time runs out.
