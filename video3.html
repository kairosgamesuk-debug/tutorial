What This Video Covers

Three types of article, three different strategies. Items are historical objects scattered randomly to past locations — carry them to HQ to earn Kairos, remove blots, and reduce the Paradox Counter. Crystals come from future space locations — deposit them at HQ for ongoing Timeline boosts every round. Artifacts are legendary relics that never leave their location — guard them in the field to protect them from Kronos and earn Kairos. Recovery uses a separate six-factor dice system distinct from travel.

The Three Article Types at a Glance

Items (Past Locations)
  • Found at random Past (historical) hexes
  • Pick up: agent must be at the same hex
  • Carry to the correct Time Location to earn Kairos & remove 1 blot
  • Each Item deposited reduces the Paradox Counter
  • Agents can carry up to 1 item (TimeLords: 2)
Crystals (Future Locations)
  • Found at Future (space) hexes
  • Carry to HQ and deposit for a Timeline boost every round
  • Crystals at HQ give flat +1 to timeline (+2 if empowered); field crystals at correct TL roll 2d6 vs 9
  • A TimeLord can Empower a crystal at HQ to boost it and nerf its paired artifact
  • 12 crystal types, each paired with one artifact at setup
Artifacts (Fixed Locations)
  • Never move — fixed at their printed hex location
  • Agent must be at the artifact's hex to guard it
  • Earn Kairos for guarding; protection blocks Kronos steal
  • If Kronos steals an artifact, it causes Timeline Control damage every round
  • 12 legendary artifacts, each paired with one crystal

Recovery Dice — The Six Specialist Factors

  • Archaeology (A): Specialist knowledge of ancient civilisations and historical context.
  • Security (S): Ability to neutralise threats and protect valuable objects.
  • Cyber (C): Technical skills for bypassing electronic locks and systems.
  • Psychic (P): Paranormal abilities useful for sensing and connecting with time artefacts.
  • Medical (M): Biological knowledge, essential for organic and living relics.
  • Historian (H): Deep knowledge of the article's origin period and cultural significance.

Each article requires a specific combination of these six factors to be recovered. Roll the recovery dice and try to satisfy all required factors within the allowed rolls. See the Articles Reference for the full list.
