What This Video Covers

Regular agents are capable but limited. TimeLords are your most powerful assets — they add a bonus die loaded with wilds to both travel and recovery rolls, carry one extra article, and unlock three exclusive actions. The upgrade triggers automatically once each agent has both accumulated 8 or more Kairos. This video covers how to earn the upgrade, what it unlocks, and why prioritising it early shapes the entire late game.

Regular Agent vs TimeLord

  • Regular Agent: Standard travel and recovery dice pools. Carry capacity: 1 article. Standard set of actions available each turn.
  • TimeLord Upgrade: Automatic — triggers the moment both unlock conditions are met during the game.
  • Bonus Die: TimeLords add one extra die loaded with wilds to every travel roll and every recovery roll.
  • Carry Capacity: Increases from 1 to 2 articles simultaneously.
  • Hazard Die Immunity: When the Main Timeline falls to -6 or below, a Hazard Die is added to travel. TimeLords are immune to its miss-turn effect.

How to Unlock the TimeLord Upgrade

Upgrade condition (per agent)
  • Agent's personal Kairos score reaches 8 or higher

Once the threshold is met for an agent, they upgrade immediately — even mid-round. Each agent upgrades individually. A 4-player game can have all four as TimeLords if the team earns Kairos efficiently across agents.

Exclusive TimeLord Actions

  • Empower Crystal: At HQ, a TimeLord can empower a deposited crystal. This gives the crystal +1 to its Crystal Light roll this round, earns +1 Kairos, and nerfs the crystal's paired artifact — Kronos skips it in the empower and Timeline Control phases for the round. (See Video 6 for full detail.)
  • Attempt the Vortex: Travel to the central Vortex hex and roll 2d6 ± modifiers. Success permanently switches off Time Collapse, stabilising the game length. Failure permanently destroys the agent. (See Video 8 for full detail.)
  • Rescue Lost in Time: An agent who is Lost in Time (LIT) cannot act normally. A TimeLord at the LIT location can use an action to rescue them, returning them to active play.
