Kronos: Your Adversary

What This Video Covers

Kronos acts at the end of every round in six ruthless sub-phases. Understanding exactly what Kronos does — and in what order — tells you precisely where to place agents, what to guard, and why certain thresholds trigger punishing cascades. This is the video that makes every other strategic decision click into place.

The Six Kronos Phases (Every Round)

  1. 1
    Steal Unprotected Artifacts

    Kronos rolls 2d6 per artifact and steals it on a total of 9 or more after modifiers: −5 if the artifact is at a non-portal location, +3 if it sits on a ring-1 location (the inner ring adjacent to the Vortex), a difficulty modifier (+2 on Hard, −2 on Easy), plus crystal excess (hoarded crystals make stealing easier), plus a timeline tier (Main Timeline ÷ 5 — a positive timeline makes stealing harder, a catch-up mechanic), −4 if Protected by an agent and −2 if Guarded. Stolen artifacts move to Kronos's possession.

  2. 2
    Empower Stolen Artifacts

    Each artifact already held by Kronos becomes empowered — amplifying the Timeline Control damage it generates in Phase 3.

  3. 3
    Timeline Control

    For every artifact in Kronos's possession, the Main Timeline drops. Empowered artifacts (Phase 2) deal larger damage. This is the primary way Kronos pushes the game toward defeat.

  4. 4
    Crystal Light (Team Benefit)

    Crystal Light uses a 3-tier system: crystals at HQ give a flat +1 to the timeline (+2 if empowered by a TimeLord). Crystals in the field at their correct Time Location roll 2d6 vs 9 — success gives +1. Crystals carried by agents or at wrong locations contribute nothing. This is the team's main counter to Kronos's Phase 3 damage.

  5. 5
    Time Collapse

    A 2d6 roll against a threshold. If it fails, the Kairos track advances — shortening the remaining game length by one round. The Vortex can permanently disable this phase if switched off.

  6. 6
    Portal Rotation

    Portals shift to new locations on the hex grid, changing which artifact sites become vulnerable to theft next round — forcing continuous repositioning of guard agents.

Strategic Implications

  • Guard priority: An artifact at a Portal hex with no agent is rolled for every round — and ring-1 sites are far more exposed (+3 to the steal roll). Always assign at least one agent to the most vulnerable artifact.
  • Kronos overflow: If Kronos steals multiple artifacts and the Timeline drops below its minimum, the game ends immediately in defeat.
  • Steal modifiers: A 2d6 roll of 9+ steals an artifact. Non-portal sites are safer (−5) and a positive timeline makes Kronos steal harder, but hoarding crystals at HQ (crystal excess) feeds Kronos's steal roll. Protecting (−4) or Guarding (−2) an artifact is the reliable defence.
  • Portal rotation forces adaptation: The artifact that was safe last round may be exposed next round. Plan movement one step ahead of the portal positions.
