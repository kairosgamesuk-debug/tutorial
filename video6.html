Empowerment, Nerfing, and Crystal Strategy

What This Video Covers

The Empower action is the most powerful defensive tool in the game — and the most overlooked by new players. Each crystal is secretly paired with one artifact at the start of every game. When a TimeLord empowers that crystal at HQ, it doesn't just improve the Crystal Light roll — it makes Kronos completely skip its paired artifact for the entire round. Discover the pairing system, when to use it, and why it's central to advanced strategy.

Crystal–Artifact Pairings

  • Random each game: At setup, each of the 12 crystals is randomly assigned to one of the 12 artifacts. The pairings differ every game.
  • Discovering the pair: When a TimeLord empowers a crystal at HQ, the game reveals which artifact it is paired with — and the nerf takes effect immediately.
  • End-of-round reset: The nerfing effect only lasts for that round's Kronos phase. The crystal must be empowered again next round to maintain protection.
  • Planning ahead: Knowing which crystal nerfs your most vulnerable artifact is the key intelligence puzzle of each game. Teams that solve it early win more consistently.

What Empowering a Crystal Does

+1 bonus to Crystal Light roll this round
+1 Kairos earned immediately
Nerf paired artifact — skips empower & Timeline Control phases

The nerf means Kronos does not empower or apply Timeline Control to the paired artifact during this round's Kronos phase. Kronos can still steal a nerfed artifact in Phase 1 if unguarded.

The Nerfing Mechanic in Detail

What "nerfed" means for Kronos Phase 1–3

Phase 1 (Steal): Kronos can still attempt to steal the nerfed artifact if it is unguarded — nerfing does not protect against theft.

Phase 2 (Empower): If Kronos already holds the nerfed artifact, it is not empowered this round.

Phase 3 (Timeline Control): Even if held by Kronos, the nerfed artifact generates no Timeline damage this round.
