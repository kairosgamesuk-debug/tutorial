What This Video Covers

Skull faces on travel dice trigger Event cards — the game's chaos engine. 40 travel events range from sending agents to Xanadu Jail to full wipes and blot additions, but also include powerful upgrades and blot removals. 18 Recovery Events fire when special faces appear during article recovery. Some cards are KEEP cards held in hand as insurance against future bad luck. The Hazard Die adds extra jeopardy when the timeline falls dangerously low.

The Three Event Types

Travel Events (40 cards)

Triggered by skull faces during travel dice rolls. Effects range from Jail, Lost in Time, and blot additions to bonus Kairos, upgrades, and blot removals.

Recovery Events (18 cards)

Triggered by special $ faces during article recovery. Separate deck with its own range of setbacks and rewards.

KEEP Cards

Some event cards are marked KEEP — hold them in hand instead of discarding. Play them at any time to counter a specific bad outcome or unlock a bonus action.

Jail and Lost in Time

  • Xanadu Jail: Agent is sent to the Xanadu hex and cannot take normal actions. Escape options: spend a turn rolling to meet a specific dice result, use a KEEP card that frees agents, or have another agent perform a Rescue action at the same hex.
  • Lost in Time (LIT): A more severe state — agent is removed from the board and cannot act at all. Only a TimeLord using the Rescue action (Video 4) can bring them back. Until rescued, the team effectively plays with one fewer agent.
  • Urgency: Both states waste turns and reduce the team's action economy. Prioritise escape before attempting new recoveries or travel.

The Hazard Die

  • Trigger: Added to all travel dice rolls when the Main Timeline drops to -6 or below.
  • Effect: If the Hazard Die shows a miss face, the agent loses their turn entirely — even if they met all four travel requirements.
  • Immunity: TimeLords are immune to the Hazard Die's miss effect (another reason to unlock the upgrade before the timeline falls).
  • Removal: The Hazard Die disappears if the Main Timeline is pushed back above -6 — crystal strategy directly counteracts it.
