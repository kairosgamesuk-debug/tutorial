What This Video Covers

How the game actually ends — and the difference between a clean win, a Pyrrhic victory, and a Kronos defeat. This final video ties together every earlier mechanic: why the early win check matters, how Time Collapse can dynamically shorten the game, what the Time Vortex switch-off gambit costs, and what the difficulty and Automa options change.

The Scoring Formula

Score = Kairos − Blots Remaining

Kairos is accumulated throughout the game via article deposits, guarding, empowering, and events.
Blots are reduced by depositing Items. Any blot still on the board at game end subtracts from your score.

Win and Loss Conditions

  • Win (Early Check): At the start of each round, before agents act, check: if all blots are cleared AND the Main Timeline is in positive territory — the team wins immediately. Timing this so the win check fires before Kronos acts is critical.
  • Pyrrhic Victory: Won, but with a low score due to remaining blots or minimal Kairos accumulated.
  • Kronos Defeat: Main Timeline hits its negative limit at any point during Kronos's Phase 3 — game ends immediately in defeat.
  • Paradox Defeat: Paradox Counter maxes out — too many undeposited items in circulation.
  • Time Defeat: The Singularity arrives — the Kairos track runs out before the win condition is met.

Time Collapse and the Vortex

  • Time Collapse (Kronos Phase 5): Each round, Kronos rolls 2d6. If the result falls below the Collapse threshold, the Kairos track advances — losing a round. High Paradox lowers the threshold, making collapse more likely.
  • Why it matters: A string of collapse rolls can eliminate several rounds from the game, turning a comfortable position into a race. Keeping Paradox low is a direct hedge against collapse.
⧖ The Time Vortex Attempt (TimeLord Only)
  • A TimeLord must travel to the central Vortex hex (costs their entire turn)
  • Roll 2d6 and add/subtract modifiers from crystals, Kairos level, and Paradox Counter
  • Success: Time Collapse is permanently disabled — Phase 5 no longer fires for the rest of the game. The primary player earns +8 Kairos and −3 Blots
  • Assist bonus: Other players with TimeLords at the Vortex who contributed to the dice modifier each receive +4 Kairos and −1 Blot
  • Failure: The TimeLord is permanently destroyed and removed from play
  • Highest risk/reward move in the game — coordinate TimeLords at the Vortex for better odds and shared rewards

Difficulty and Game Options

Easy

Higher MLT starting point, lower Kronos steal thresholds (non-portal modifier still applies), more starting Kairos.

Normal

Standard settings as designed. Recommended for learning the full system.

Hard

Lower MLT start, tighter Collapse thresholds, fewer starting resources.

Automa

Solo / reduced player count mode. An automated agent fills missing player slots.
